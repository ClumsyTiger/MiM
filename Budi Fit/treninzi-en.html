YOGA


Yoga is an ancient method of self-development originating from India, which involves the use of techniques: physical postures (Asana), exercises to manage prana (energy) through breathing (Pranayama), relaxation-meditation exercises (Pratjahara), concentration (Dharana), meditation (Dhyana) and self-realization (Samadhi). By practicing yoga techniques, you can achieve greater emotional balance, absence of expiriencing deprivation and destruction, self-satisfaction, sense of meaning of life, knowledge of one's own way of reacting - by focusing on events in one's body, slowing down and deep breathing, harmony of breathing and movement, physical and mental relaxation.

PILATES


Pilates is a sophisticated form of exercise that simultaneously develops specific muscle strength as well as flexibility of muscles and joints, thus achieving optimal body balance. Pilates elongates and tones body, impacts elegant posture, relieves stress, contributes to better self-control and better self-confidence. Pilates largely avoids high impact, high power output, and heavy muscular and skeletal loading.

CORE


Abdominal exercises are exercises designed to strengthen the muscles, tendons and ligaments of the abdomen. These exercises, along with back exercises, are key to maintaining the body's torso. A well-maintained torso (abdominal and back muscles that surround the spine) is one of the best ways to maintain good posture as you age and one of the best defenses against frequent back injuries that cause pain. Building and maintaining a healthy torso is best done as part of a daily routine or integrated into an accompanying exercise program.

CARDIO


Cardio is a type of training that combines rhythmic aerobic exercises with stretching and strength exercises. The goal is to increase the strength, elasticity and cardiovascular capacity of the organism. It is performed in groups, with music and the presence of an aerobics instructor. Each group is designed for a specific level of experience and is taught by a certified instructor with a special area related to their specific class. The number of calories burned depends on the speed of movement, the height of the steps, the length of the exercise and the height and weight of the person.
